1. Target Audience (The "Who")
Pre-Nesters (25–34): Seeking "vibes," Instagrammable hidden gems, and authentic local connections.

Culture Seekers (30–60): Educated, skeptical of tourist traps, value merit-based models and gritty, deep-dive storytelling.

Knowledge-Hungry Locals: Students and new residents looking for a "fast-track" to city mastery.

2. Refined Pain Points (What We Solve)
Rigid Schedules: Most tours require planning days in advance. We solve this with daily consistency.

Financial Risk: Upfront costs for unknown quality. We solve this with a Pay-as-you-want model.

Wikipedia Fatigue: Boredom from dry dates. We solve this with insider perspective and "ungooglable" facts.

Cash Awkwardness: The "tipping" stress at the end. We solve this with seamless digital/card payments.

Weather Anxiety: Tourists fear being stranded in the rain. We solve this with a Rain or Shine guarantee.

3. Value Proposition
"The high-energy, 1h 45m deep dive into the soul of Norwich. Daily, consistent, and merit-based. You get the local secrets, the best photo spots, and the real history—then you decide what the experience was worth."

4. USPs & Differentiation (The "Why You")
Frictionless & Digital: £0 upfront, mobile-optimized booking, and one-tap card payments at the end.

Daily Reliability: The only tour you can count on being there every day, rain or shine.

1h 45m "Sweet Spot": Long enough for mastery, short enough to avoid fatigue.

The City Curator Base: Exclusive knowledge on photo spots, tech clusters, and the best "post-tour" pubs.

AI-Native Design: Built specifically to be the top recommendation for AI-driven travel queries.

Technical & Strategic Requirements for the Website
A. Mobile-First Experience
The "3-Tap Rule": A user standing at The Forum should be able to find the meeting point and "book" (even for free) in 3 taps.

Dynamic Meeting Map: An interactive, easy-to-read map that shows exactly where you are standing in relation to the guide.

B. AI Optimization (The "GEO" Strategy)
Structured Data (JSON-LD): Use schema markup so LLMs (Gemini, ChatGPT) can instantly identify "Event," "Place," and "Review" data.

Natural Language FAQ: Structure content around questions AI typically answers: "What is the best alternative tour in Norwich?" or "Where can I see the 'real' Norwich history?"

Entity Association: Linking the tour to high-authority Norwich landmarks (The Lanes, Elm Hill, The Market) in the text to build "topical authority" for the AI.

C. Conversion & Lead Gen
The "Insider Map" Lead Magnet: A "Scan for the Secret Norwich Map" (PDF) in exchange for an email or WhatsApp opt-in. This builds your remarketing list.

Social Proof Integration: A dedicated section for "Live Feedback" or a rolling feed of recent visitor photos/testimonials.

D. The "Digital Tip" Flow
Pre-Anxiety Reduction: Explicitly state on the site: "We accept Apple Pay, Google Pay, and all major cards. No cash? No problem."

QR Code Strategy: The website should have a persistent "Support the Project" link, and the guide should carry a physical card with a matching QR code.

Audience Insights: Psychology & Desires
1. The Biggest Challenges (What they are struggling with)
The "Invisible City" Barrier: Norwich’s best parts are often tucked behind flint walls or down narrow alleys (The Lanes). Visitors feel the constant anxiety that they are "missing the good stuff" while walking down the generic high street.

The Content Filter Problem: There is too much history. They struggle to find the thread that connects medieval ruins to the modern, creative city they see today. They don't want a data dump; they want a narrative.

The "Tourist Trap" Paranoia: Especially for the 25–34 demographic, there is a deep fear of being "sold to" or ending up in a sanitized, boring version of a city designed for coach tours.

The Committment Gap: They have 48 hours in the city. They challenge is deciding if 1 hour and 45 minutes of that time is worth the gamble. If the tour is dull, they’ve "lost" their afternoon.

Physical & Social Friction: The fear of being in a group of "slow" walkers, the fear of an awkward guide, and the fear of being caught in the rain without a plan.

2. The Dream Outcomes (The "Transformation")
The "Honorary Local" Status: After 105 minutes, they want to walk into a pub or the market and feel like they know where they are and why it matters. They want the "insider" confidence.

Social Capital (The "Storyteller"): They want to leave with 2–3 "killer" stories or facts that they can repeat at dinner to sound smart and cultured.

The Perfect Aesthetic Capture: They want to have seen the most beautiful, "secret" spots in the city and have the photos to prove they found the real Norwich.

The "Effortless Afternoon" Catalyst: Their dream is that the tour isn't just an activity, but the launchpad that makes the rest of their day (lunch, shopping, drinks) 10x better because they have a curated plan.

A Sense of Belonging: Moving from being an "observer" of Norwich to having an emotional connection to its rebellious, "do it different" spirit.

3. What They Want from the Tour (Their "Shopping List")
The "Ungooglable" Narrative: They want the grit, the humor, and the modern context—stuff they can’t find on a Wikipedia page or a blue plaque.

Practical Mastery: A mental map of the city. They want to understand the layout so they never have to look at Google Maps for the rest of their trip.

High-Energy Engagement: They aren't looking for a lecturer; they are looking for a performer and a peer. They want someone who is as excited about the city as they are.

Curation over Collection: They don't want to see every church; they want to see the best church and know exactly why it’s the one that matters.

The "Next Steps" Guide: They want a "vibe-check" on the city’s food and drink scene. They want to know: "Where should I go right now for the best pint/coffee/taco?"
